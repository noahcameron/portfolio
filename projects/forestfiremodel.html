Forest Fire Spread Model

  • Tools/Methods: ArcPy, ArcPro, Multiple Regression
  • Full Project Paper: Project Link

Created a model to simulate the spread of forest fires.

As a part of a team for the final geograhic informations systems project at the University of Waterloo, researched the spread of forest fires, and the impact of different mitigation methods, and created a model to simulate the spread of a forest fire.

The model is an ArcPy tool that takes a tesselation as input, with info about the geography that are relevant to forest fire spread (wind speed, elevation, precipitation, temperature, etc). These factors and their weight on how they impact spread was determined through a Multiple Linear Regression applied to historical forest fire data.

At each stage the model uses the MLR results to estimate the probabilty that fire spreads from one cell to each of its neighbours. The probability is used with RNG to simulate if it spreads or not. The fire continues to spread until all cells fail to spread to a neighbour, and then the total spread area can be determined.
